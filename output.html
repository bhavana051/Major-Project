Virat Kohli

Early Life and Background:

Virat Kohli was born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, India. He grew up in a middle-class family and displayed a keen interest in cricket from a young age.

Rise to Prominence:

Kohli's talent was evident from the start, and he quickly rose through the ranks in Delhi's cricket circles. He captained the Indian U-19 cricket team to victory in the 2008 ICC U-19 Cricket World Cup.

Consistent Performances:

Over the years, Kohli has been one of the most consistent performers in international cricket. He has amassed numerous records and accolades, including being the fastest player to reach 8,000, 9,000, 10,000, and 11,000 runs in ODI cricket.

Captaincy Stint:

Kohli took over the captaincy of the Indian cricket team in Test cricket in 2014 and in limited-overs formats in 2017. Under his leadership, India achieved significant success, including historic Test series wins in Australia and a runner-up finish in the 2017 ICC Champions Trophy.
